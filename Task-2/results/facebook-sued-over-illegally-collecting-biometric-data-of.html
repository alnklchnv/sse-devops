Facebook sued over 'illegally collecting biometric data' of Instagram users

Joanna Estrada
August 13, 2020
Share

The photo-centric platform stands accused of collecting, storing, and profiting from biometric data of more than 100 million users without their consent, according to Bloomberg.

Facebook is being sued for allegedly collecting the biometric data of Instagram users or people who appear in photos on the platform without their consent. Users were only made aware of biometric data collection on Instagram at the start of this year, but it is believed to have occurred for some time before that. As the report notes, Facebook has already offered to pay up $650 million in order to settle a similar lawsuit claiming it illegally collected biometric data using a photo-tagging tool for Facebook.

According to Bloomberg, the practice violates an IL privacy law and prohibits the unauthorized harvesting of biometric data. Per that law, Facebook could face fines of $1,000 per violation - or $5,000 if it's found to have acted recklessly or intentionally. The settlement increased global concern over "biometric privacy".
